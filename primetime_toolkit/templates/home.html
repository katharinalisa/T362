What is the "Prime Time"?

Bec Wilson defines Prime Time as the period in life, typically between ages 47 and 65, when individuals experience significant transitions in career, family, health, and personal aspirations. During this stage, people often focus on planning for retirement, maintaining wellbeing, and exploring new interests, while navigating changes in work, supporting adult children, caring for ageing parents, and reassessing financial priorities. Embracing Prime Time means recognising the value of experience and using it to make informed decisions about the next phase of life, building resilience, strengthening relationships, and pursuing personal growth for a fulfilling journey into retirement and beyond (Wilson, 2025).

What is the Prime Time Toolkit?

The Prime Time Toolkit is a comprehensive resource designed to help Australians aged 47–65 navigate the complexities of retirement planning. It offers a range of tools, resources, and expert insights to empower individuals to take control of their financial future.

By using the Prime Time Toolkit, you can:

  • Assess your current financial situation and set realistic retirement goals.
  • Use interactive tools to estimate your retirement savings and future income.
  • View calculations showing the impact of superannuation and investments.
  • See how retirement drawdown affects your income and savings over time.
  • Get a clear overview of your assets and liabilities to understand your net worth.
  • Review your regular expenses and see how they influence your retirement budget.
  • Track upcoming bills and payments to help you stay organised and avoid missing deadlines.

The goal is to accumulate enough savings to provide an income in retirement. Superannuation is typically compulsory for employees, with employers required to contribute a percentage of an employee's salary to their super fund.

Retirement drawdown refers to the process of withdrawing funds from your retirement savings to cover living expenses during retirement. It involves strategically managing your assets to ensure a sustainable income stream while minimizing the risk of depleting your savings too quickly.

What is Superannuation?

Superannuation is a way of saving for retirement in Australia. It’s a long-term investment that helps you build a nest egg for your future. Contributions to your superannuation fund are generally made by your employer, and you can also make additional voluntary contributions. The money is then invested on your behalf, with the aim of growing your retirement savings over time

Understanding how superannuation works is crucial for making the most of your retirement savings. Superannuation funds offer a range of investment options, allowing you to choose how your money is invested based on your risk tolerance and retirement goals. Over time, the power of compounding returns can significantly increase your super balance. It's also important to keep track of your super accounts, as having multiple accounts can lead to unnecessary fees. Consolidating your super into one account may help you save on costs and manage your retirement savings more effectively.

How Superannuation Works

  • Employer Contributions: Most employers are required to pay a percentage of your earnings into your super fund. This is known as the Superannuation Guarantee.
  • Voluntary Contributions: You can add extra money to your super through salary sacrifice or after-tax contributions to boost your retirement savings.
  • Investment Growth: Your super fund invests your contributions in assets like shares, property, and cash. The returns help grow your balance over time.
  • Accessing Super: Generally, you can access your super when you reach retirement age or meet certain conditions.
  • Tax Benefits: Superannuation offers tax advantages to encourage saving for retirement, making it a tax-effective way to build wealth.

It’s important to regularly review your superannuation, understand your fund’s fees and investment options, and consider seeking financial advice to make informed decisions about your retirement savings.


Tips for Maximising Your Superannuation

  • Consolidate Your Accounts: Combine multiple super accounts to reduce fees and simplify management.
  • Review Investment Options: Choose investments that match your risk profile and retirement goals.
  • Check Insurance: Many super funds offer insurance; review your coverage to ensure it meets your needs.
  • Update Your Details: Make sure your contact and beneficiary details are current.
  • Compare Fees: Compare fund fees and performance to ensure you’re getting value for money.
