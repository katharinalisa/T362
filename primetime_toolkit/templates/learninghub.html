What is Prime Time?

Prime Time refers to the period in life, typically between ages 47 and 65, when individuals experience significant transitions in career, family, health, and personal aspirations. During this stage, people often focus on planning for retirement, maintaining wellbeing, and exploring new interests, while navigating changes in work, supporting adult children, caring for ageing parents, and reassessing financial priorities. Embracing Prime Time means recognising the value of experience and using it to make informed decisions about the next phase of life, building resilience, strengthening relationships, and pursuing personal growth for a fulfilling journey into retirement and beyond (Wilson, 2025).

What is the Prime Time Toolkit?

The Prime Time Toolkit is a comprehensive resource designed to help Australians aged 47–65 navigate the complexities of retirement planning. It offers a range of tools, resources, and expert insights to empower individuals to take control of their financial future.

By using the Prime Time Toolkit, you can:

  • Assess your current financial situation and set realistic retirement goals.
  • Use interactive tools to estimate your retirement savings and future income.
  • View calculations showing the impact of superannuation and investments.
  • See how retirement drawdown affects your income and savings over time.
  • Get a clear overview of your assets and liabilities to understand your net worth.
  • Review your regular expenses and see how they influence your retirement budget.
  • Track upcoming bills and payments to help you stay organised and avoid missing deadlines.

Explore by Topic

Superannuation is a way of saving for retirement in Australia. It involves making regular contributions to a super fund, which is then invested to grow over time. The goal is to accumulate enough savings to provide an income in retirement. Superannuation is typically compulsory for employees, with employers required to contribute a percentage of an employee's salary to their super fund.

Retirement drawdown refers to the process of withdrawing funds from your retirement savings to cover living expenses during retirement. It involves strategically managing your assets to ensure a sustainable income stream while minimizing the risk of depleting your savings too quickly.

Super growth refers to the strategies and approaches aimed at maximizing the growth potential of your superannuation savings over time. This can include investment choices, contribution strategies, and understanding the impact of fees and taxes on your superannuation balance.
